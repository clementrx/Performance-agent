# Program v1 — 10K under 50:00 in 21 weeks

**Athlete:** ~1 year of consistent running, 3 sessions/week available.
**Current benchmark:** 10K in 55:12 (5:31/km, race last month).
**Goal:** 10K in 49:59 (5:00/km), 21 weeks out.

**Feasibility (engine):** probability **84%**. Improvement needed 9.5%, i.e.
0.45%/week required, against ~1%/week achievable at this training age. Comfortable
margin — the plan can absorb a missed week or two.

## Skeleton — model & justification

- **Model:** periodization waves over 21 weeks — 3-week build ramps, deload every
  4th week, 2-week taper. A roughly two-week taper cutting volume 41–60% while
  holding intensity is the best-supported pre-race strategy (Bosquet 2007, ★★★☆☆).
- **Session mix:** one interval day, one tempo day, one long run. Both interval and
  continuous endurance training produce large VO2max gains, with somewhat greater
  gains from intervals (Milanović 2015, ★★★☆☆).
- **Not included:** a strength add-on may improve running economy (Llanos-Lagos 2024,
  ★★★☆☆) but the athlete has exactly 3 days — volume priority. Revisit if a 4th day
  opens up.
- **Monitoring:** every session logged as duration × RPE (session-RPE load,
  Foster 2001, ★★☆☆☆ — a practical method, used with its caveats).

## The baseline week

**Day 1 — Intervals** *(purpose: VO2max — Milanović 2015, ★★★☆☆)*
15 min easy warm-up · 6×800 m @ 4:55–5:05/km, 400 m jog recovery · 10 min cool-down.

**Day 2 — Tempo** *(purpose: threshold — coaching judgment)*
15 min easy · 20 min continuous @ 5:20–5:25/km · 10 min easy.

**Day 3 — Long run** *(purpose: aerobic base — coaching judgment)*
70 min @ 6:20–6:45/km. Conversational effort, RPE ≤ 4.

Deload weeks: same three sessions at 0.6× volume (4×800 m · 12 min tempo · 45 min
long), intensity eased to 0.9×. Taper weeks: 0.5× volume, paces unchanged
(Bosquet 2007, ★★★☆☆).

## Tests & check-ins

- **5K time trial** in weeks 8 and 16 (fresh after the deload): the engine re-predicts
  the 10K time and paces are re-derived from the result.
- **Check-in every 2 weeks:** logged sessions vs. plan, session-RPE load trend,
  triggers watched (missed sessions, RPE drift, pain → program-adaptation).
