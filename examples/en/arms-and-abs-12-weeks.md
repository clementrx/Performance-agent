# Program v1 — Visible abs & bigger arms in 12 weeks

**Athlete:** 1.78 m, 82 kg, ~2 years of lifting, 4 sessions/week available.
**Goal:** visibly bigger arms and outlined abs in 12 weeks (recomposition).
**Nutrition frame v1:** ~2,400 kcal/day (moderate deficit), 164 g protein/day
(2.0 g/kg) — set by the Nutritionist skill with its safety guards.

**Honest framing:** in 12 weeks both goals can progress, neither can be maximized —
recomposition is slow at two years of training. Arms respond to volume; abs appear at
low body fat, which the deficit drives. If starting body fat is above ~18%, "outlined"
by week 12 is unlikely and the coach says so at every check-in.

## Skeleton — model & justification

- **Model:** periodization waves over 12 weeks — deloads in weeks 4 and 8; week 12 is
  a light week with final measurements.
- **Volume:** clear dose-response between weekly hard sets and muscle growth
  (Schoenfeld 2017, ★★★☆☆). Arms are the priority: 12 → 16 hard sets/week each for
  biceps and triceps; other muscles held at maintenance volumes.
- **Frequency:** each muscle trained 2×/week; frequency matters mainly as a way to
  distribute volume (Grgic 2018, ★★★☆☆).
- **Progression:** double progression — fill the rep range, then add load
  (ACSM position stand, Kraemer 2002, ★★★☆☆).
- **Core:** a progressive structured core block twice a week (Başandaç 2025, ★★★☆☆) —
  abs are *revealed* by the deficit but still *trained* for thickness.
- **Deficit rule:** heavy compound work stays in the plan to protect strength while
  cutting — coaching judgment.

## The baseline week

Prescriptions are sets × reps @ RIR (reps in reserve). Loads for the big lifts are
computed by the engine from the athlete's lift inventory; isolation loads follow the
rep range.

**Day 1 — Upper A (arm priority)**
- Bench press 3×6–8 @ RIR 2 · Row 3×8–10 @ RIR 2
- Incline curl 3×8–12 @ RIR 1–2 · Overhead triceps extension 3×8–12 @ RIR 1–2
- Lateral raise 2×12–15 @ RIR 1

**Day 2 — Lower + core**
- Squat 3×5–8 @ RIR 2 · Romanian deadlift 3×8–10 @ RIR 2
- Leg press 2×10–12 @ RIR 2
- Progressive core block 10 min (Başandaç 2025, ★★★☆☆)

**Day 3 — Upper B (arm priority)**
- Weighted pull-ups 3×6–8 @ RIR 2 · Overhead press 3×6–8 @ RIR 2
- Bar curl 3×8–12 @ RIR 1–2 · Cable pressdown 3×8–12 @ RIR 1–2
- Hammer curl 2×10–12 @ RIR 1 · Skullcrusher 2×10–12 @ RIR 1 *(from week 5)*

**Day 4 — Conditioning + core**
- 30 min easy cardio (zone 2) — supports the deficit, coaching judgment
- Progressive core block 15 min · abs 3×10–15 weighted

Progression rule on every exercise: double progression — reach the top of the rep
range on all sets at the given RIR, then add load ~2.5% (Kraemer 2002, ★★★☆☆).

## Measurements & check-ins

- **Weekly:** bodyweight (same conditions), arm circumference every 2 weeks.
- **Weeks 8 and 12:** waist measurement + photos — the honest indicator for "abs".
- **Check-in every 2 weeks:** logged sessions vs. plan, load progression on curls
  and pressdowns, bodyweight drift vs. the nutrition frame; strength dropping on the
  big lifts → deficit reviewed with the Nutritionist skill (program-adaptation).
