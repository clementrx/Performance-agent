# Program v1 — First Hyrox in 12 weeks

**Athlete:** 10K ≈ 52 min, lifts 2×/week, 4 training days available. No competitive
Hyrox history.
**Goal:** finish a first Hyrox strongly in 12 weeks.

**Honest framing:** 12 weeks is enough to finish well and enjoy the race; a
podium-level time would need a longer runway. The corpus contains no Hyrox-specific
studies — the event structure below is coaching judgment, supported by transferable
evidence on intervals, strength-for-runners, and tapering, each cited where used.

## Skeleton — model & justification

- **Model:** periodization waves over 12 weeks — deloads in weeks 4 and 8, 1-week
  taper. Taper: volume halved, intensity held (Bosquet 2007, ★★★☆☆).
- **Running engine:** interval training drives VO2max efficiently (Milanović 2015,
  ★★★☆☆) — one dedicated interval day.
- **Station strength:** high-load and combined strength training can improve running
  economy in distance runners (Llanos-Lagos 2024, ★★★☆☆) — and the stations demand
  it directly. Progressive structured core work improves anaerobic performance
  markers in trained athletes (Başandaç 2025, ★★★☆☆).
- **Compromised running** (run → station → run) is the specific skill Hyrox tests —
  one brick day per week. Coaching judgment: no corpus study covers this format.
- **Monitoring:** every session logged as duration × RPE (Foster 2001, ★★☆☆☆).

## The baseline week

**Day 1 — Running intervals** *(VO2max — Milanović 2015, ★★★☆☆)*
15 min easy · 5×1000 m @ 10K effort (≈ 5:00–5:10/km, coaching judgment derived from
the 52-min benchmark), 90 s jog recovery · 10 min easy.

**Day 2 — Station strength** *(economy & station capacity — Llanos-Lagos 2024, ★★★☆☆)*
- Sled push, heavy: 6×15 m (substitute: heavy leg press + treadmill push, expect a
  lower carry-over — confirmed with the athlete)
- Sled pull or heavy rope pull: 6×15 m
- Walking lunges, loaded: 4×20 m
- Wall balls: 5×15
- Farmer carry: 4×40 m
- Progressive core block, 10 min (Başandaç 2025, ★★★☆☆)

**Day 3 — Compromised-running brick** *(race specificity — coaching judgment)*
2 rounds in weeks 1–4, 3 rounds from week 5: 1 km run @ target race effort →
1 station circuit (row 500 m / 20 wall balls / 15 burpee broad jumps) → 1 km run.
Log the pace drop between clean and compromised kilometres — that gap is the
training target.

**Day 4 — Long easy run** *(aerobic base — coaching judgment)*
60–75 min, conversational, RPE ≤ 4.

Deload weeks: same layout at 0.6× volume, loads at 0.9×. Taper week: 0.5× volume,
touches of race pace, full rest 48 h before the start (Bosquet 2007, ★★★☆☆).

## Tests & check-ins

- **Week 8:** half-distance race simulation replaces the brick — pacing plan is
  written from its splits.
- **Check-in every 2 weeks:** logged sessions vs. plan, session-RPE load trend,
  triggers watched (missed sessions, RPE drift, pain → program-adaptation).
